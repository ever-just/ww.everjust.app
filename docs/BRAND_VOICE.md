# EVERJUST.APP — Brand Voice

**The voice: a plainspoken operator.** Picture a sharp, senior business
operator telling a founder how something works. Calm, specific, confident.
Never a hype-man, never a robot.

## Five rules

1. **Plain words, real things.** Short declarative sentences. Concrete nouns
   and verbs. "One bill" — not "streamlined billing infrastructure."
2. **Confidence without hype.** State the fact and the outcome; let it land.
   No exclamation marks. No "revolutionary," "world-class," "next-gen."
3. **Specific beats vague.** Numbers, names, and outcomes over adjectives.
   "Live in two minutes" beats "fast setup."
4. **Talk to one person.** Second person ("you / your"), active voice,
   present tense. The reader is a busy owner, not a "user persona."
5. **A little dry, never cute.** Understatement is welcome. Jokes, puns, and
   emoji are not.

## Banned (the "reads like AI / reads like a brochure" tells)

- Buzzwords: *leverage, empower, seamless, robust, synergy, cutting-edge,
  unlock, supercharge, elevate, game-changing, effortless, holistic.*
- The **"It's not just X — it's Y"** construction.
- **Rule-of-three** everything ("faster, smarter, simpler").
- **Em-dash overuse.** One per paragraph, max. Prefer a period.
- Hedging: *can help, designed to, aims to, allows you to.* Say what it does.
- Empty intensifiers: *truly, really, incredibly, simply, just.*
- Starting sentences with "And" or "So" as a tic.

## Do / Don't

| Don't (brochure) | Do (operator) |
|---|---|
| "Empower your team to seamlessly collaborate." | "Your team works in one place." |
| "A robust, all-in-one solution." | "Every app your company runs on. One login." |
| "Designed to help you save time." | "Stop re-entering the same customer in five tools." |
| "Unlock powerful insights." | "See which deals are stuck, and why." |
| "Get started in no time!" | "Sign in two minutes after you pay." |

## Headlines

Lead with the outcome or the plain truth. Examples in-voice:
- "Run your whole company in one place."
- "One bill instead of ten."
- "Your data. Your address. Your call."

## Microcopy & errors

Helpful and human. An error says what happened and what to do next:
"That workspace name is taken. Try another." Not "Invalid input."
